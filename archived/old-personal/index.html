I am a computer science student specializing in software engineering at the University of Toronto.

In high school, I started playing around with front-end web development and then stumbled upon the back-end. What started out as a project to build a website to post homework due dates for fellow classmates ended up bringing me to the world of "Hello, World!". As years progressed, I learned multiple languages during my free time or in classes. Listed on the left (for desktop) or above (for mobile) are some of the languages that I have learned over the years and my comfortability with them. Please note that not all the languages that I know are listed because I have dealt with a wide variety of languages for many different projects. Not only that, but I believe my ability to learn a syntax quickly would make me able to use almost any language thrown my direction.

From coding in my bedroom and into the classroom at the University of Toronto, it is evident that there is a reason why I have stuck to it. That reason is the ability to make something useful and maybe even world-changing with some key strokes is mind-blowing. Plus, it can be admitted that the basic computer science logic relates back to life in such a coincidental way. For example, life is full of if and elses, as well as life being full of loops (either mistakes or experiences). With this, I believe computer science and understanding life is correlated (and I sure do like to understand life).
